## 원문

Long context didn’t kill chunking. It made bad chunking more obvious.
Chunking is the preprocessing step where long text gets split into smaller pieces before being embedded, stored, and retrieved, especially in RAG and document-heavy workflows.
And that makes sense.
Once model performance gets strong enough, a lot of the next bottleneck starts to shift toward retrieval and context organization.
Chunking used to feel like a small backend detail.
Now it’s becoming much more visible as part of system performance.
You can see that shift in the tooling too.
IBM directly ties chunking strategy to retrieval accuracy, and Google is building more content-aware chunking into its RAG stack.
A lot of what looks like model performance is also system design:
how information is packaged, retrieved, and passed into context.
Chunking is just one visible example.
A lot of the work is now moving into the layers around the model - data infrastructure, retrieval, access to fresh information, and the way context is actually built.
