## 원문

I've seen dozens of governance pitches die in the same way.
Too many slides. Too much theory. An ask that's too big. And not a single number from the actual organisation.
The pitches that get funded? They fit on one slide.
Here's what goes on it:
What it costs us today. Real numbers. From your org. Not benchmarks. Not industry stats. Yours.
→ 412 analyst hours last quarter recreating metrics that already exist
→ Two conflicting revenue numbers in the March board deck
→ Three weeks to answer a due diligence question that should take a day
→ One AI pilot paused because nobody could confirm the training data
That's what you're already paying for governance. You're just getting nothing for it.
What changes in 90 days:
→ Top 10 metrics defined, owned, and published
→ One source of record declared per metric
→ Board questions answered same day
→ AI deployment unblocked with governed data
What it requires:
→ One governance lead. Not a department.
→ 2 hours per week from five domain owners
→ Tools you already bought but never configured
Keep the ask small. That's where most pitches die. They ask for too much.
Nobody funds governance because it's important.
They fund it because the cost of not having it is on the slide.
