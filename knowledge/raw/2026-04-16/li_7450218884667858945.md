## 원문

We are running the largest uncontrolled experiment in cognitive delegation in human history, and almost nobody is naming it clearly.
OutSystems research released this month. 96% of enterprises have deployed AI agents. 94% are already concerned about agent sprawl — autonomous systems making decisions across the business with nobody fully accountable.
Gartner expects 40% of enterprise applications to include task-specific AI agents by the end of this year.
The infrastructure question is loud. The psychological one is quieter and more important.
For the first time in history, we are delegating not just tasks but judgement, on a timeline shorter than the cognitive adjustment required to understand what we just gave away.
There is a concept in psychology called source monitoring. It is the brain's ability to track where a thought originated. Did I decide this? Did I read it somewhere? Healthy source monitoring is how you know you are thinking your own thoughts.
When people spend hours every day with assistants that shape their framing and complete their sentences, source monitoring degrades. Early cognitive-offloading research points to reductions in recall and in the felt sense of authorship. You begin to believe an idea is yours when it was suggested, refined, and handed back to you by a model.
Now multiply that by an enterprise deploying thousands of agents. Each review becomes shallower as trust grows. Within a few quarters, a company runs on judgement it did not form and cannot fully audit.
PwC showed this year that roughly 20% of companies capture about 75% of the economic gains from AI. Everyone assumes the winners have better tools. I have sat in enough rooms across London and Dubai to tell you this is not what I see.
The winners have preserved decision coherence at the top. They deploy agents aggressively, but they protect the layer of judgement that requires lived context, ethical weight, and long-horizon thinking. They treat AI as leverage for clarity they already have. Not as a substitute for clarity they never developed.
The losers are scaling delegation faster than they are scaling self-awareness. They feel productive. They are slowly losing the map.
Autonomy outsourced before self-awareness is developed does not make you efficient. It makes you invisible inside your own company.
If you cannot articulate in plain language what your agents are deciding on your behalf, you do not have an AI strategy. You have an abdication disguised as one.
What decisions in your business, right now, are being made on your behalf that you could not reconstruct if asked?
