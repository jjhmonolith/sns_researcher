Building a business today means producing a huge amount of content.
Not just the obvious stuff like webinars, blog posts, social clips, interviews, and product demos.
There’s also all the internal content that keeps things moving:
→ async walkthroughs
→ team updates
→ internal demos
→ product explanations
At some point, content stops being a side task and starts becoming part of how the business runs.
The hard part is not always coming up with the content.
It’s the production mess around it.
You record in one tool. Edit in another. Fix audio somewhere else. Cut clips. Export. Re-export. Rename files. Do small fixes that somehow eat half the day.
A lot of the friction is not creative.
It’s operational.
That’s why I’ve been paying more attention to AI tools that remove workflow bottlenecks, not just generate more output.
That’s also where Descript makes sense.
What I like about it is that it simplifies a workflow that usually gets scattered across too many tools:
→ record
→ edit by changing text
→ clean up mistakes
→ publish
All in one place.
That matters more than people think.
Because the real challenge with content is usually not making one good piece.
It’s being able to turn that piece into usable output quickly and consistently.
A webinar becomes clips.
A podcast gets cleaned up fast.
A walkthrough can be updated without starting over.
That’s the part that actually makes content sustainable.
Not just more ideas.
Not just more effort.
Just fewer steps between recording something and getting it into use.
