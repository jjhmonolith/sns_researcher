## 원문

Founder Series #2
GIGR Inc. (dba. Playad) is building an AI marketing team powered by multi-agents. I had a chance to connect with the GIGR Inc. co-founders Youn Hwang & Arthur Koo at their SF office and hear how they’re thinking about go-to-market.
1. Marketing is built on uncertainty
Every marketer is asking the same questions:
Which creative will resonate?
Which channel will convert?
Which growth lever will scale?
The answer is always the same. Test everything.
The problem is that testing is expensive, slow, and never-ending. The cycle repeats. Look at what worked, create variations, test, find winners, scale, then repeat when performance drops. Marketers already know what to do. Execution is the bottleneck.
Right now, their focus is on significantly reducing that cost of uncertainty.
Over time, the goal is more ambitious—to eliminate that uncertainty altogether through systems that learn from performance and continuously improve.
2. Finding your playbook takes “맨땅에 헤딩”
There are playbooks in go-to-market. The hard part is figuring out which one works for your company.
As they put it, it often feels like “맨땅에 헤딩” learning through repeated tests and trial and error until something clicks.
What they’re building is aimed at reducing that process so companies don’t have to figure everything out the hard way.
3. Building for founders who don’t have the bandwidth
They are working closely with founders across different stages, especially teams that don’t have the bandwidth to focus deeply on marketing.
The goal is to help them move from manual execution to making decisions. Less time operating, more time thinking about what works.
4. A clear philosophy behind how they work
The company name GIGR stands for “Giver’s Ground.”
They talked about wanting to work with people who genuinely care about helping others and creating value, especially for the companies they support.
5. Why SF?
Environment and density. People who are naturally motivated. Like-minded builders. A shared focus on building something bigger. That combination is hard to replicate.
Appreciate the conversation with the GIGR team!
Curious how others are thinking about making marketing more predictable.
