## 원문

An interesting signal right now: Agents behavior is becoming insurable.
We’re starting to see AI systems treated more like operational infrastructure with measurable risk.
Besides the capabilities, once these systems move into real-world workflows, we also start caring about how they fail.
That’s what makes this move from ElevenLabs interesting.
They’ve become the first company to go live with an AIUC-1-backed insurance policy covering AI voice agents.
Before an agent can be insured, it goes through 5,000+ adversarial simulations across areas like security, safety, reliability, data privacy, and accountability.
So the bar is not just whether the system works in a demo.
It’s how it behaves under pressure, across edge cases, attacks, and mistakes.
The second layer is even more interesting:
the insurance is tied to the agent’s behavior itself.
So if an AI voice agent gives incorrect information and that leads to financial damage, there is now a more formal framework for how that risk gets evaluated and insured.
More on AIUC-1 certification https://lnkd.in/giiWNgNC
The next phase of AI adoption will also be about testing, certification, and whether risk can be defined clearly enough for companies to actually operate these systems at scale.
Curious how others here see it -
do certification and insurance actually change how seriously you take AI agents?
