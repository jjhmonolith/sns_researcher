## 원문

Ok y'all, I know I am a day late on giving the beat on the new priorities from the US Department of Education, but here's my take.
Following the Trump administration’s move to cut the U.S. Department of Education’s staff by nearly 50%, the agency has revealed a significant restructuring of its core programs. New priorities announced this week signal a shift in how the department’s remaining resources will be utilized, emphasizing a transition of power to the states and a departure from the "equity-based" frameworks of previous administrations.
The department’s new direction centers on three primary pillars: Career and Workforce Readiness, Artificial Intelligence integration, and Literacy. This overhaul is being executed largely through a new interagency agreement with the Department of Labor, which now oversees several key programs formerly managed by the Education Department.
Let's go first to Career and Workforce Readiness
The Department has finalized a "workforce-first" approach, officially moving Career and Technical Education (CTE) under the purview of the Labor Department.
The new priorities focus on:
Local Alignment: Tailoring education to meet specific regional industry needs and the skilled trades.
Apprenticeships: Expanding pre-apprenticeships and dual-enrollment programs.
Educator Pipeline: Following public feedback, the department added a specific focus on paid educator apprenticeships to address national teacher shortages and streamline the path to certification. Shout out to The Pathways Alliance
Artificial Intelligence: Innovation Without Federal Oversight
Despite the Office of Educational Technology's closure last year (a bad idea), the administration is aggressively pushing AI literacy to ensure students remain competitive. While the department acknowledged public concerns regarding student privacy and safety, it has explicitly declined to set federal guardrails. (Congress, anyone?)
Key Goals: Integrating AI into K-12 curriculum, providing professional development for teachers, and creating AI certification tracks for high schoolers.
State-Led Regulation: The department maintains that safety and privacy policies are "optimally decided at the state and local level."
Literacy and the Shift from DEI
The department is launching grant competitions specifically aimed at advancing literacy in high-need districts. These grants support:
Library Enhancements: Updating school library materials and digital resources.
Early Intervention: Focusing on early language development in low-income communities.
Notably, while these grants target "high-need" and "low-income" areas, the administration has framed this as a move toward academic merit rather than social equity.
