## 원문

LG just put its first open-weight VLM on Hugging Face — EXAONE 4.5
Built to work across both text and images, especially for document-heavy, real-world tasks.
Interesting one partly because it is coming from a large industrial research organization.
The whole release feels heavily oriented toward actual use, and built with real-world systems and workflows in mind.
A few notes I put down here - in case helpful:
𝟏. 𝐏𝐫𝐚𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐜𝐚𝐥: it defines its strengths and boundaries pretty clearly
EXAONE 4.5 explicitly leans into document understanding, and even includes recommended settings for OCR and document-related tasks.
That gives the whole thing a more practical feel, especially for scanned pages, structured docs, charts, and mixed-format materials that show up in real workflows.
𝟐. 𝐃𝐞𝐩𝐥𝐨𝐲𝐦𝐞𝐧𝐭 / 𝐬𝐞𝐫𝐯𝐢𝐧𝐠 / 𝐟𝐫𝐚𝐦𝐞𝐰𝐨𝐫𝐤 / 𝐡𝐚𝐫𝐝𝐰𝐚𝐫𝐞 𝐠𝐮𝐢𝐝𝐚𝐧𝐜𝐞
It reads more like a usable open model, not just a stronger one.
The model card does not just talk about capability. It also includes deployment and serving info, long-context support, framework compatibility, and hardware guidance.
It also helps that they are talking about inference speed too - multi-token prediction, and roughly 1.5× faster decoding than the previous model.
That combination makes it feel closer to something builders can actually plug into workflows.
𝟑. 𝟐𝟔𝟐,𝟏𝟒𝟒 𝐭𝐨𝐤𝐞𝐧𝐬 𝐨𝐟 𝐜𝐨𝐧𝐭𝐞𝐱𝐭
On its own, that is not the headline anymore.
But in a model clearly leaning into document understanding, it feels more meaningful.
Less like a broad “multimodal” claim, more like something built for longer, denser, multi-page, structured content.
