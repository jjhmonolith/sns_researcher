## 원문

46% of workers who already have AI tools available at their company refuse to use them.
Their reason, from the Gallup survey published this month: "I prefer to keep doing my work the way I do it now."
That sentence is not about technology. It is about identity.
The brain does not resist new tools. It resists threats to the self-image it has spent decades constructing. If you have built a career on being "the person who does X well," anything that rewrites how X is done is not a software update. It is an identity audit.
This is why retraining programmes fail. Why excellent tools sit unused. Why half of the workforce now uses AI at work while the other half watches from behind an invisible wall.
Clinical psychology has a term for this pattern. Ego-syntonic behaviour. Actions that feel consistent with who you believe you are. When a tool threatens that consistency, the mind will manufacture a thousand rational-sounding reasons to reject it. The reasons feel true. They are post-hoc.
The leaders winning the AI transition are not the most technical. They are the ones whose identity was never fused with the specific way they used to work.
They did the harder job first. They separated who they are from what they do.
AI is not the test. It is the moment the test becomes visible.
What would you have to stop being, in order to become who this decade requires?
