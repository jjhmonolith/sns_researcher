## 원문

One thing I keep noticing across a lot of EdTech products right now is how quickly they’re becoming impressive… and how slowly they’re becoming reliable.
AI can generate content instantly, personalize journeys, answer questions, simulate tutors. From the outside, everything looks like progress.
But if you sit inside these systems for a while, a different pattern shows up.
The experience changes a lot depending on the learner.
For one user, it works brilliantly.
For another, it breaks quietly.
Wrong difficulty.
Irrelevant suggestions.
Confusing feedback.
And because everything is happening dynamically, it’s hard to even trace where it went wrong.
So teams keep adding more intelligence to fix it.
More data.
More prompts.
More layers.
But the system doesn’t necessarily become more dependable. It just becomes more complex.
That’s the trade-off that doesn’t get talked about enough.
AI makes things more powerful, but also less predictable.
And in education, unpredictability is not a small issue.
Because you’re not just building a product.
You’re influencing how someone learns, what they trust, and how confident they feel progressing.
If that experience is inconsistent, the learner feels it even if the metrics don’t show it clearly.
So maybe the real shift isn’t about how intelligent these systems can become.
Maybe it’s about how consistent they can remain while doing it.
Because in learning, trust is built on consistency, not just capability.
So the question is:
as AI makes learning systems more dynamic, how do we make sure they don’t become less dependable?
