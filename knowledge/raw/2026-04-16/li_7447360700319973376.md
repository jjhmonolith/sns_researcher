## 원문

Districts are drowning in edtech tools and fragmented curriculum right now, and AI has only added to the clutter. Teachers are using seven different platforms. Admins have no visibility into what's happening or who is actually using the HQIM they invested years of time & millions of dollars into. Boards are starting to ask questions nobody has answers to yet.
I get it. I've been on both sides. I taught social studies in Minneapolis Public Schools. I also served on the MPS school board, where I saw firsthand how technology decisions actually get made at the district level: the budget conversations, the stakeholder buy-in, the board politics.
Now I work at Brisk Teaching, where I help districts across the U.S. implement AI that teachers actually use, with the visibility and curriculum alignment that administrators need to feel confident saying "yes."
Brisk's newest solution, Curriculum Intelligence, is the layer that unifies fragmented edtech tools and vetted curriculum with district-designed guardrails. Think of it as the control tower districts have been asking for.
If your district is figuring out its AI strategy, I'd love to compare notes.
Happy to connect: josh@briskteaching.com
