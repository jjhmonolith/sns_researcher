## 원문

Stop pitching governance as something the business should care about.
They won't.
Show them what it costs them right now not to have it. In language they already use.
Money.
Without: four teams maintaining four versions of the same metric. Each rebuild costs analyst time nobody tracks.
With: one certified definition built once. Reused everywhere. Rebuild cost drops to zero.
Ask your analytics team how many hours they spent last quarter rebuilding something that already existed.
Time.
Without: the board asks a data question. Three weeks of finding, validating, reconciling before anyone can answer.
With: same question. Answered same day. Source, definition, and owner already documented.
Measure how long it takes today to answer a simple executive question. That gap is the ROI.
Risk.
Without: AI trained on a table nobody verified. Regulator asks how a decision was made. Nobody can answer.
With: every model input traced to a governed source. Audit question answered with a link, not a project.
Ask legal how long the last compliance question took to resolve. Governance cuts that by months.
Trust.
Without: CFO and VP Sales show different churn numbers in the same meeting. Nobody knows which is right.
With: one number. One definition. One owner who signs off. The meeting is about what to do, not which number to believe.
Count how many times last quarter someone asked "where did this number come from."
Each one is a trust failure.
Nobody funds governance because it's important.
They fund it because the cost of not having it is on the slide.
