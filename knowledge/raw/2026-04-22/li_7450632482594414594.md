## 원문

The debate is over.
AI is in the room.
Last week, Hong Kong Filmart, 30 years running, 8,000 industry professionals, 53 countries, held the most important conversation in cinema right now.
There were no talks about budgets or distribution.
It was whether the humans in the building still understood what they were building for.
28 panels devoted entirely to AI. An AI Academy. An AI Hub. Asia's screen industry arrived having already decided that the technology belongs in production.
While Hollywood is still locked in union negotiations and legal grey zones, Asia showed up with tools, workflows, and finished work.
Speaking of finished work, South Korean broadcaster MBC premiered Raphael at the market. 80 minutes. Entirely AI-generated. A feature.
The room watched it.
Then Peter Chan, one of Asia's most commercially formidable directors, stood at a panel and said what most people in the industry are thinking but won't say out loud:
"Those days of the blockbusters are gone. AI won't be an enemy to auteur film. It will be an enemy to mediocre blockbusters."
Sit with that sentence for a moment.
He was surgically separating two things the industry has always conflated, spectacle and storytelling. Scale and soul. The first is now automatable. The second never was.
Google's AI specialist on the same panel put it plainly:
"The most important thing is that you have to unlearn your past experiences and learn to work in new ways."
The professionals who survive this transition will be the ones who know what AI cannot replicate, which is the exact, unrepeatable texture of a human perspective rendered with enough honesty that a stranger in a dark room feels less alone.
That's always been the job. The tools just changed.
Are you keeping up?
