## 원문

Happy World Creativity and Innovation Day.
The Innovation Wall.
15 meters. 4 rows. 300+ projects. 4 years. PreK to Grade 12. Every subject. No kits.
This wall represents a deliberate shift from teaching content to building a culture of innovation.
Building this required more than resources. It required establishing a vision, designing the framework, training and leading the educators who delivered it, and creating a culture where every subject and every grade level became a space for innovation.
Teachers were trained. Spaces were equipped. Expectations were redefined. Systems were built to sustain it.
From carpentry and welding to 3D printing and microcontrollers, the infrastructure supported one goal: students creating real, meaningful work from scratch. No kits. No shortcuts. All hands. All scratch.
Every subject became a design challenge. Arabic language students built 3D maps and handcrafted books documenting the journeys of historic travelers. Music students built functional guitars and pianos powered by micro:bit microcontrollers. Science students built water ecosystems, urban communities, and light and shadow installations. Every lesson ended with something real.
No two projects were ever the same. If the brief was to build a car, every team built a different car from a different idea using different resources. Individuality and creativity were non-negotiable.
Through innovation days, STEAM cycles, and structured project-based learning, ideas consistently moved from concept to final presented deliverable. High school students worked as mentors and supervisors for younger learners. Cross-grade collaboration, accountability, and public presentation were built into the model from the start.
Creativity was not optional. It was embedded.
What began with foundational projects evolved into students designing autonomous systems and electric vehicles. The students who built cell models in grade 4 became the engineers who built autonomous rovers and electric cars in grades 11 and 12.
As AI continues absorbing theoretical knowledge at scale, the ability to build, create, and deliver with your own hands remains irreplaceable.
The Innovation Wall is not a display of projects. It is proof of a system, a vision, and a culture built intentionally.
