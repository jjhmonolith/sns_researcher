I mapped where AI actually delivers for DevOps — and where the gap between demo and production is widest.
5 domains. Maturity-rated. Updated for April 2026.
→ AIOps: Dynatrace's Perform 2026 agentic AI claims 12x better success rates than LLM-only. Datadog launched an MCP Server for AI agent integration. New Relic's SRE Agent reports 25% faster resolution. The constraint? All of them amplify messy observability hygiene. Clean your tagging first.
→ Self-Healing: 60%+ large enterprise adoption expected by year-end. K8s native self-healing is mature. Everything else still has human approval gates. And that's the right call for now.
→ LLM-Assisted IaC: Terraform MCP Server v0.4 added Stacks and Sentinel support. Pulumi Neo cut provisioning from 3 days to 4 hours at Werner Enterprises. IaC copilots are real — infrastructure reasoning engines are not.
→ Agent Orchestration: CrewAI hit v1.11. LangGraph is GA with durable execution. The pattern that works: agents handle diagnostics, humans decide. Full autonomy? Still experimental.
→ FinOps for AI: 98% adoption. #1 skillset teams need. GPU costs don't follow CPU optimization patterns. The FinOps Foundation's AI working group is where to start.
The organizations winning aren't chasing full autonomy.
