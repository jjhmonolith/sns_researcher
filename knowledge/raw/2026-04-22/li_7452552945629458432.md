## 원문

53% of the world now uses generative AI.
Took three years. The PC and internet were both slower to reach that number. (Stanford's 2026 AI Index, out last week.)
Singapore? 61%. Highest adoption rate on the planet.
But adoption isn't the same as integration.
Most business owners have tried ChatGPT. Asked it to write an email. Maybe a social caption. Then went back to doing things the old way.
That's not using AI. That's tourism.
Meanwhile, the cost of running these models dropped 280x in under three years. From US$20 to $0.07 per million tokens. 88% of organisations globally are now using AI in at least one business function.
The gap isn't between people who've heard of AI and people who haven't.
It's between people who've built it into how they work — and people who opened ChatGPT once and said "okay lah, interesting."
The internet had a long runway. You could show up in 2005 and still build a decent website. AI compounds differently. People who start now will be so far ahead in 12 months that everyone else won't be competing — they'll be playing catch up.
One thing you can do today: pick the task you hate most. Customer replies. Product descriptions. Meeting notes. Run it through AI with a proper prompt. Time yourself before and after.
That's it. No masterclass needed. Just start.
What's the one task you'd hand off to AI first?
