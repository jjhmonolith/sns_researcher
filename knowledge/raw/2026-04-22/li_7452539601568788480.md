## 원문

Last week I had the opportunity to attend the first few days of Semafor World Economy in Washington, DC — 450+ CEOs and government leaders from across every sector spanning the G20 economies as well as top minds in corporate governance.
A few noteworthy takeaways:
1. AI capability is outpacing governance - and boards are accountable. The question boards need to ask is not whether AI is in your operations - it is who is accountable when it fails.
2. “Workflow surgery,” not AI overlay. The distinction between being a “tourist” vs being a “resident” with AI should reframe how boards evaluate management’s transformation plans. Applying AI to step 4 of an existing process is not transformation.
3. Employment disruption is inevitable. Boards should be asking what is management’s workforce transition strategy and does it go beyond cost reduction?
4. Energy infrastructure is a material business risk. Data center energy demand is growing exponentially. State-level regulatory backlash is already here. Capital allocations that assume unlimited, affordable compute access require fresh scrutiny.
5. Regulatory fragmentation is the compliance crisis hiding in plain sight. For multi-state operators the myriad and varying state AI bills introduced and enacted are no longer merely a policy watch item. The issue belongs on the risk register.
A special highlight for me was participation in a small group session of board chairs and lead directors on the topic of The Future of Capitalism. What role does board leadership play in supporting and challenging the CEO and management team in this era of increasing uncertainty?
Special thanks to Calvin Yee and the entire SEMAFOR team for the invitation.
It was also a pleasure to connect with fellow board universe colleagues Daria Walls Torres, Joe Hurd, Avril Ussery Sisk NACD.DC, Bonita Stewart, Lakecia Gunter,
Lindsay Trout and my dear friend and fellow Horace Greeley High School and Brown University alum, Heidi Messer, among others.
I look forward to returning next year.
