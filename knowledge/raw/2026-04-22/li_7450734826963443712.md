## 원문

Nobody's job title will be 'prompt engineer' in five years.
But everyone's job will require knowing how to work with AI.
That gap between where your team is today and where they need to be?
That is the real digital transformation project.
Not the software. Not the budget. The people.
Here is what companies getting this right are doing differently:
➡️ THEY ARE RESKILLING FROM THE INSIDE OUT
Not flying in consultants to train everyone once
then leaving a slide deck nobody reads.
They are identifying internal champions —
the curious ones, the early adopters —
and giving them the space and resources to go deep.
Then those people pull everyone else forward.
➡️ THEY ARE MAKING AI USE PART OF DAILY WORK
Not a separate initiative.
Not a quarterly workshop.
If using an AI tool is not part of how someone does their job today it will not be part of how they do it tomorrow either.
Integration beats education every time.
➡️ THEY ARE HAVING HONEST CONVERSATIONS ABOUT ROLES
Some tasks will disappear. That is true.
The companies that pretend otherwise
lose their best people to the ones being transparent.
Your team can handle the truth.
What they cannot handle is finding out you knew and said nothing.
➡️ THEY ARE MEASURING CAPABILITY, NOT JUST COMPLETION
Did your team finish the AI training module?
That is not the question.
Can they apply it to solve a real problem next Monday?
That is the question.
Training without application is just attendance.
The uncomfortable truth is this:
Most organisations are investing in AI tools and underinvesting in AI-ready people.
The tool is the easy part.
The human transition is where the real work is.
And the leaders who understand that are the ones their teams will still respect
three years from now.
What is one thing your organisation is doing to genuinely prepare its people for AI-augmented work?
