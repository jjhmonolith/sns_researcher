## 원문

I've been watching enterprise teams struggle with Claude Code for the past three months.
The gap isn't technical capability. It's how companies are onboarding their teams.
Most orgs treat AI coding tools like any other SaaS rollout:
Send the docs. Schedule a 1-hour demo. Hope developers figure it out.
That doesn't work with Claude Code.
Here's what actually moves the needle:
Workshop-style onboarding with real production use cases.
Not toy examples. Actual code from the team's backlog.
Pairing senior engineers who've shipped with Claude alongside teams who haven't touched it yet.
The handoff isn't docs — it's muscle memory.
Building company-specific eval frameworks during onboarding.
How do you know if Claude's refactor is production-ready? Your CI/CD doesn't know yet. Build that muscle early.
Teaching prompt engineering as a core skill, not an afterthought.
The difference between a junior dev with strong prompting and a senior dev with weak prompting is shrinking fast.
The companies seeing 30-40% faster iteration cycles?
They're not the ones with the best infra or the biggest AI budgets.
They're the ones who invested in structured, hands-on learning before they shipped a single AI feature to prod.
If you're rolling out Claude Code (or any AI coding tool) to your team — how are you training them?
