## 원문

Most founders don’t hit a growth ceiling because demand dries up.
They hit it because their best people are doing $30/hour work at $180/hour salaries.
That’s the quiet profit leak I keep seeing.
On paper, the business looks “busy”. In reality, senior capacity is being chewed up by approvals, status chasing, rework, and handoffs that should never need founder-level attention.
You feel it as:
• slower delivery even when everyone is flat out
• squeezed margin with no obvious blowout
• constant escalation back to you for decisions your team should own
If this sounds familiar, run this 3-part capacity reset:
• Time (Reclaim It): list the top 5 recurring tasks your senior people touched last week. Circle anything repeatable. Remove one repeat loop immediately.
• Team (Upskill Them): assign one owner for each critical workflow. If ownership is vague, escalation returns to the founder every time.
• Technology (Align It): map where work gets re-keyed or duplicated. One broken handoff can quietly drain hours across the whole week.
Most businesses try to fix this with one new tool. That rarely works.
Capacity is a system issue, not a software issue.
When Time, Team, and Technology are aligned together, founders stop being the pressure valve for every bottleneck.
You get decision space back, the team moves faster, and margin usually improves without adding headcount.
Where is your biggest capacity leak right now: repeat admin, unclear ownership, or broken handoffs?
