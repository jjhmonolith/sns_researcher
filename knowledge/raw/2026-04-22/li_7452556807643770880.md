## 원문

Data center investments are underwritten on land, power, and connectivity. Capacity is benchmarked in MW and valued accordingly. That assumption holds only if installed MW converts into monetizable compute capacity.
Over the last three decades, rack densities have moved from 3–10 kW to 50–120+ kW. Infrastructure has not evolved at the same pace. With AI, HPC, and high-density workloads, air-cooled facilities encounter thermal limits that constrain deployable capacity within the same power envelope. What starts to show up is not compute limitation, but thermal constraint, including:
– Inability to support higher rack densities without derating
– Increasing share of power consumed by cooling
– Lower effective compute yield per MW
These do not present as operational failures. They reflect as underutilized or stranded capacity against the underwritten MW.
This is where the shift happens, from engineering metrics to financial outcomes. Lower usable capacity per MW directly impacts:
– Revenue per MW (lower)
– Cost per unit compute (higher)
– Infrastructure utilization (constrained)
– ROI / IRR (compressed)
This is where asset class differentiation begins. Facilities designed with immersion-based cooling remove airflow dependency and enable higher density within the same power and space envelope. This directly impacts:
– Higher monetizable compute per MW
– Improved revenue per rack and per MW
– Optimized infrastructure utilization over asset life
– Stronger ROI / IRR profile
The evaluation lens shifts, and the questions asked are:
– What portion of installed MW converts to billable compute?
– What is the revenue yield per MW under high-density workloads?
– How does cooling architecture impact ROI and IRR over the asset lifecycle?
These are not design considerations, but underwriting variables. At this point, validation under real workloads i.e. a POC becomes relevant. Not to test cooling, but to establish capacity realization, revenue yield, and performance consistency at higher densities.
Cooling is no longer in the CTO’s domain alone, but it is now a CFO variable. It determines asset performance, yield, and scalability. Immersion-cooled infrastructure represents a distinct and emerging data center asset class.
