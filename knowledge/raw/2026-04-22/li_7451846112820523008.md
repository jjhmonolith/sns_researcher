## 원문

For the first time in tech, speed is no longer a competitive advantage.
For decades, “speed to market” was everything. We optimized for it. Measured it. Rewarded it.
That just changed.
AI has commoditized velocity.
Startups and enterprises can now:
→ Prototype in hours
→ Ship in days
When everyone is fast…
👉 Speed becomes the baseline.
The real bottleneck?
Judgment.
We’re entering an era of high-velocity hallucination
— where bad decisions get built, deployed, and scaled before anyone realizes they’re wrong.
As an Enterprise Architect, I see the shift clearly:
It’s no longer about how fast you build
It’s about what you choose to build.
The real risks now aren’t obvious:
→ Choosing what’s easy for AI to generate vs. what’s right for the business
→ Scaling architectures that haven’t been stress-tested
→ Introducing dependencies you don’t fully control
(I’ve already seen teams rush into full rebuilds, where a Strangler approach would have reduced risk and avoided months of rework.)
The uncomfortable truth:
In a world moving at AI speed,
the most valuable person in the room…
is the one who knows when to slow things down.
Because:
→ Fast + wrong = expensive
→ Fast + scalable + wrong = catastrophic
We don’t need more people who can build faster.
We need people who can think clearly under acceleration.
Because in the AI era:
👉 Your Judgment Quotient (JQ) matters more than your velocity
