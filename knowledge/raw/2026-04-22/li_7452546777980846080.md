#Singapore are not behind on AI because of budget.
They are behind because nobody has made the decision to actually begin.
Analysis paralysis is the silent killer of digital transformation.
And it is dressed up in very respectable language.
'We are still evaluating our options.'
'We want to wait for the technology to mature.'
'We are planning a comprehensive strategy first.'
Meanwhile, a competitor who started small and imperfectly six months ago is already on version two.
Here is what I have observed separates the companies moving forward from the ones still in the planning phase:
➡️ THEY ACCEPT THAT THE FIRST VERSION WILL BE WRONG
Perfection is the enemy of progress in AI adoption.
The companies winning are not smarter.
They are more comfortable with iteration.
They ship something imperfect, learn from it, and improve.
That cycle is faster than any planning document.
➡️ THEY GIVE ONE PERSON REAL AUTHORITY TO MOVE
Committees do not transform organisations.
Empowered individuals do.
Find the person in your company who is already
experimenting with AI tools on their own time.
Give them a small budget, a clear outcome, and get out of the way.
➡️ THEY TREAT FAILURE AS DATA, NOT EMBARRASSMENT
A pilot that did not work is not a failed project.
It is six months of learning that your competitor has not paid for yet.
The organisations that punish early failure
guarantee they will always be late to every next thing.
➡️ THEY SEPARATE 'READY' FROM 'PERFECT'
You will never have clean enough data.
You will never have full buy-in across every department.
You will never have zero risk.
Waiting for perfect conditions to begin is choosing guaranteed stagnation
over manageable uncertainty.
The truth is simple and uncomfortable:
The cost of starting imperfectly today is almost always lower than the cost of starting perfectly eighteen months from now.
Your team is ready enough.
Your data is good enough.
The only thing missing is the decision.
What is one AI or automation initiative your company has been 'almost ready' to start for longer than six months?
