## 원문

Every AI vendor claims their solution is "intelligent" and "grounded." But when you're evaluating conversational AI for enterprise customer service, those buzzwords don't pay the bills or reduce ticket volume.
I've watched too many enterprise software buyers get dazzled by demos, only to face reality when their AI chatbot hallucinates product information or can't handle anything beyond basic FAQs. The problem? They didn't ask the hard questions up front.
Here's what separates marketing hype from real AI capability. First, demand clarity on deterministic guardrails. LLMs are probabilistic by nature, which means they guess. How does the vendor prevent hallucinations when your customer asks about pricing, returns, or account-specific issues? If they can't articulate their guardrail architecture, that's a red flag.
Second, understand the role of human expertise in the system. AI doesn't magically understand your business processes, product catalog nuances, or compliance requirements. Ask how domain experts review, correct, and continuously improve the AI's performance. If the answer is vague or relies solely on "the model learns over time," you're looking at a solution that will require constant firefighting.
Third, challenge the model size assumption. Vendors love to tout their use of the largest, most powerful LLMs. But for most customer service workflows, a right-sized model that's been properly trained on your specific use cases will outperform a generalized giant while costing significantly less to operate. Ask them to justify why bigger is actually better for your specific requirements.
Finally, dig into what unique value they bring beyond the underlying LLM. Anyone can build a wrapper around GPT or Claude. The real question is: what domain-specific intelligence, integration depth, and workflow automation capability does this vendor provide that you couldn't get elsewhere?
The AI vendors who can answer these questions with specificity and confidence are the ones worth your time. The ones who deflect or double down on buzzwords? Keep looking.
