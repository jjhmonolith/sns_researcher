## 원문

Excited to share that our latest work, "From Tokens to Layers," has been accepted to MLSys 2026 and will be presented next month!
Serving Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models with massive context windows is exposing a critical flaw in current stall-free schedulers. The standard approach—chunked prefill—partitions prompts by tokens. However, in MoE architectures, this forces redundant expert weight loads, creating a severe memory bottleneck and eroding the very sparsity benefits that make MoE efficient.
Our solution is Layered Prefill.
Instead of slicing the input sequence horizontally, we vertically partition the model into contiguous layer groups. By interleaving prefill and decode across these layer groups, we maintain stall-free decoding while entirely eliminating chunk-induced MoE weight reloads.
The impact on performance and efficiency:
- Up to 70% lower Time-to-First-Token (TTFT)
- 41% reduction in end-to-end latency
- Up to 22% lower energy consumption per token
Huge congratulations and thanks to my incredible co-authors who drove this research: Gunjun Lee, Jiwon Kim, Jaiyoung Park, and Younjoo Lee.
Read the full paper to see how shifting the scheduling axis from tokens to layers unlocks a new operating regime for high-efficiency, energy-aware MoE serving.
