## 원문

(Slightly edited as of Mar 3rd, 2026)
Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) has long been considered too computationally intensive for real-time applications. I am thrilled to share two recent papers from our lab (IEEE Micro and ISPASS 2026) that challenge this assumption, demonstrating sub-25ms inference using CKKS on a single consumer GPU.
1. Achieving the DARPA DPRIVE Milestone on a Gaming GPU:
We achieved sub-25ms (specifically, 22.4ms) CNN7 inference using the CKKS scheme. This performance milestone was widely perceived to require a custom ASIC costing tens of millions of dollars to develop. Instead, we accomplished it using a single NVIDIA RTX 5090. By building on our prior work—Cheddar, a SOTA performant GPU implementation of CKKS, and AESPA, which bypasses costly bootstrapping by approximating activation functions with square polynomials (requiring a slight, strategic modification to the original CNN7 specification without sacrificing accuracy)—and introducing a new encrypted convolution method inspired by HyPHEN, we have brought real-time FHE to accessible hardware.
🔗 Preprint: https://lnkd.in/gS-6RY2p
2. Breaking the Persistent Memory Wall in GPU-based CKKS:
To surpass our own SOTA performance (Cheddar @ ASPLOS 2026), we analyzed on-chip cache behavior and identified that dominant CKKS kernels remain strictly bound by memory bandwidth, even with a high-bandwidth L2 cache. Our proposed solution, Theodosian, tackles this persistent memory wall through: a) an L2-aware multi-polynomial batching strategy, b) complementary pipelining that executes DRAM-bound and L2-bound kernels concurrently, and c) exploiting CUDA Graphs by leveraging the static structure of FHE workflows.
The result is a bootstrapping time of just 15.2ms—a more than 20x reduction compared to our 2021 record of 328ms.
🔗 arXiv (Camera-ready in progress): https://lnkd.in/gRqhQJTG
I am immensely proud of my co-authors (Hyunah Yu, Hyesung Ji, Jongmin Kim, Jaiyoung Park, Sangpyo Kim, and Wonseok Choi) for their collective effort on this remarkable endeavor. Through rigorous understanding, analysis, and optimization of FHE across CPUs, GPUs, and ASIC proposals, we have transformed real-time FHE from a theoretical concept into a reality.
