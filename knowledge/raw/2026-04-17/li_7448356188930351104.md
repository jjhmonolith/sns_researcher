## 원문

When I think of AI in schools, I think of opportunities for personalised learning. Having been a teacher, I remember the struggle of ensuring progress for every student. Teachers often have students with broad range of abilities and personal targets — stretch and diagnostic questions help to tailor lessons to different needs, but bigger classes mean bigger differences in need.
This makes it genuinely harder to personalise learning. I found myself spending a lot of time supporting lower-ability students, while higher-ability students were often left to work independently with little more than an extra worksheet.
With AI-powered digital notebooks, I could have stretched those students far beyond just additional homework.
That said, I do have mixed feelings. AI in the classroom risks becoming just another thing teachers have to log into — another account, another platform, another thing students need to remember. That reduces productivity rather than improving it.
This is exactly where good AI governance, careful planning, and long-term thinking are essential..
