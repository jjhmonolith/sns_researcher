## 원문

Yesterday the First Lady entered a White House Summit on AI in Education with a robot.
IMHO something else was more interesting: it was the word she used.
“Personified”
The first lady said "AI's future is personified." She meant humanoid robots. I think the word should also mean something else.
Like many of you, I’m following the many states banning devices in classrooms. I think it stems from something real: we’re seeing a real crisis with kids and screens. You don’t need to read Jonathan Haidt to see it. Children need more human connection. More social skills.
So ‘personified’ must mean more than a robot or a Chromebook in front of students.
It has to center educators. They are the true agents of change in a classroom.
It should mean that AI is personalized to teachers: it’s grounded in your context as an educator. Your curriculum. Your students. Your classroom. Not generic intelligence — grounded intelligence.
I've seen this movie before. The hype around AI in education reminds me of the "personalized learning" wave I lived through at Chan Zuckerberg. Massive energy. Massive investment. The search for a magic bullet made it easy to default to more screen time — rather than empowering the educators already in the room.
We can't make that mistake again.
The version I believe in — the one 2M+ teachers are already using — is AI that works behind the scenes.
A teacher who shows up Monday morning more prepared. Better plans. Sharper differentiation. More time for the human moments no device can replace.
Not more screen time. More room for the stuff that matters.
That's personified.
What do you think?
