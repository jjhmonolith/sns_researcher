## 원문

I’ve been hearing a lot of the same question from CTOs and VPs of Engineering lately: what does “AI ROI” actually mean in practice?
In many large companies, the immediate challenge is still adoption—where to apply AI, how to roll it out, and how to make it stick. But in teams where AI is already part of everyday development, the questions shift. Leadership, investors, and stakeholders start asking: Did we actually get faster? Did quality improve? Are we seeing benefits beyond the cost?
The tricky part is that “productivity” is a moving target. The impact of tools changes quickly, and what matters most this quarter might not be the same next quarter.
And even when teams feel development has sped up, release speed doesn’t always follow.
As AI increases the volume of changes, the load often shifts downstream—PR review capacity, CI/CD throughput, and production stability start to carry the constraint.
So when we talk about AI ROI, I don’t think it’s enough to measure cost savings or code generation speed. I’d rather look at a few practical signals:
Where work waits in reviews and handoffs, whether quality issues are caught before merge, what true end-to-end cycle time looks like, and how change volume correlates with operational stability.
If we don’t measure those things, it’s hard to locate the bottleneck—and even harder to improve it.
For teams that have adopted AI: where did your bottleneck move, and what are you using to track it?
