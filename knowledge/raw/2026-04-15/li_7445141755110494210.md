## 원문

I didn’t look back much after graduating college. I just kept moving forward.
At Moloco, I spent seven years building and scaling—fast, loud, adrenaline-fueled years. For a long time, I thought that was what life was supposed to feel like.
Then one evening, I found myself alone in the office after everyone had left. For the first time in years, there was silence. And I realized I had been running at a relentless pace for a long time—not because anything was wrong, but because I didn’t know how to slow down.
So I stepped away.
Over the past few years, I traveled, played a lot of golf—a slow, quiet, humbling sport—and spent time in stillness. It gave me the space to reset and reconnect with myself.
Recently, I started to miss something again. Not just the pace, but the shared energy of building something with a team moving in the same direction. The kind of energy where a small team can build something extraordinary—like we did at Moloco, growing the business 500x in 7 years.
So, I’m jumping back in. Back to building.
But this time, I’m returning with something different. I’m no longer relying on adrenaline to carry me, but on a sense of calm and clarity that I can hold onto, even in the middle of the storm.
I’m excited to share that I’ve joined FriendliAI as Chief Business Officer, where I will lead our global business and operations. We are at a major inflection point in AI. As Jensen said in his GTC keynote, inference is the new frontier. For the past five years, the FriendliAI team has been building cutting-edge inference optimization technology, and today we’re delivering industry-leading inference performance for AI-native companies. If you are building AI products and running inference at scale, we would love to work with you.
It’s good to be back.
