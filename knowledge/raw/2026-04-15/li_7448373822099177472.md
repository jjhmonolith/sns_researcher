## 원문

Imagine renting a car without speaking to a single human.
That was my experience with Orcar in Korea 🇰🇷
From landing to unlocking the car took under an hour.
🚫 No queues.
🚫 No paperwork.
🚫 No language barrier.
As a foreign traveller, that last part stood out. Everything happened through the app. Verify your licence and IDP, find the car, take a quick photo, a selfie, wait briefly and it unlocks 🔓
It felt almost too easy.
The experience was clean and intuitive. No counters. No confusion. No translation needed. Returning the car was just as simple.
What stood out most was not the app itself, but what sat behind it. Identity checks, coordination and operations were all handled digitally. This is what AI looks like when it works. Not a feature you click, but infrastructure you do not notice.
Orcar is not your traditional car rental company. It is a car sharing company for international travelers. Run by just five people, including three engineers, it has already expanded into Japan and the USA and relies heavily on AI to scale operations end to end 🚀
More broadly, this is something I keep noticing across Asia. These are not experiments, they are real systems reducing friction, especially for foreigners.
So what is holding Europe back?
This experience was part of a collaboration with Orcar, but all opinions are my own.
