## 원문

Most AI failures don’t start with bad models.
They start with invisible breaks in the system.
Everything looks fine.
The data flows.
The dashboards update.
The outputs make sense.
Until they don’t.
What actually breaks first is harder to see:
• Data moves without context
• Ownership becomes assumed, not explicit
• Definitions drift across teams
• Workarounds become the process
None of these stop the system.
They distort it.
So decisions still get made.
But they’re shaped on assumptions no one is actively checking.
By the time the problem becomes visible,
the decision has already been made.
That’s the gap most teams miss.
They try to fix outcomes.
But the real leverage sits earlier:
Not in the model.
Not in the dashboard.
In the structure that defines:
• what data is trusted
• who owns it
• when a decision is allowed to happen
• and when it should be stopped
If those constraints aren’t explicit,
the system doesn’t fail loudly.
It fails gradually… and convincingly.
The question isn’t:
“Is the AI working?”
It’s:
“What conditions are shaping the decisions before we even see them?”
