## 원문

We've built over 30 AI tools for teachers in the last three years. And the biggest problem we face right now isn't building the next one, it's that teachers can't find the ones we've already made.
That's not a Brisk problem, it’s an AI-in-education problem.
Every edtech company is racing to build features. Feedback tools, quiz generators, lesson planners, differentiation engines, intervention trackers. The list keeps growing. But we've created a world where teachers have to know what exists before they can use it. They have to navigate menus, remember feature names, sit through PD sessions that walk them through option C under tab B.
That's not how anyone actually thinks when they're teaching.
A teacher doesn't think "I need to open the intervention recommendation engine." They think "half my kids with the quiz, what do I do now?" A curriculum director doesn't think "let me cross-reference the scope and sequence alignment tool." They think "are our teachers actually teaching what we agreed on?"
The gap between how educators think and how AI tools are organized is where most of the friction lives. Not in the AI itself, but in the architecture around it.
I've been thinking a lot about what it would mean to just ask teachers one question: what do you need? And then figure out the rest. No menus. No training on where to click. Just a starting point that meets them in their language, not ours.
That sounds simple. It isn't. It means the system has to understand intent, not just input. It has to know whether "quiz" means create one from scratch or pull up the one you made last Tuesday. It has to know when to move fast and when to slow down and ask a clarifying question the way a good co-teacher would.
The AI companies that win in education won't be the ones with the most features. They'll be the ones that make those features disappear into the work itself.
For the educators in my network: what's the bigger barrier to using AI in your classroom right now?
🔵 I don't know what tools are available
🟢 I know the tools but can't find what I need inside them
🟣 I don't have time to learn another platform
