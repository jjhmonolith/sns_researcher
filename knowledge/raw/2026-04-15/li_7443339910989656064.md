## 원문

It's National AI Literacy Day. Hot take:
We don't need AI literacy classes. We need AI literacy everywhere.
Dedicated AI courses are fine. But if that's the whole strategy, we've already lost.
Matt Miller nails this in his new book AI Literacy in Any Class. Best AI literacy happens in 30-second moments inside the classes teachers already teach.
- Spanish teacher asking kids to spot weirdness in AI images.
- History class predicting what ChatGPT will say then ripping the response apart.
- A math teacher projecting a Brisk Boost activity to their class in order to give them a chance to collaborate on problems and get feedback.
He calls it "embedded AI literacy." Exactly right.
Same thing is true for AI tools. The industry keeps building AI that lives outside the teacher's workflow. Separate app. Separate login. Separate from the curriculum. Then acts surprised when nobody uses it.
AI literacy doesn't need its own period. AI tools don't need their own tab. Both need to show up where the work already happens.
That's Brisk.
