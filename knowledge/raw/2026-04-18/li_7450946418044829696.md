#ClaudeDesign just dropped an update.
As a UX/UI designer, here's my honest take.
📌 How I tested it
1. Opened Apple's official iOS/iPadOS design system file in Figma
2. Exported the design system as JSON
3. Loaded it into Claude Design System and asked: "Create a login page"
It picked up the design system and built the login page right away.
💥 But midway through... Usage Limit hit.
Turns out Claude wasn't just making a login page — it had gone ahead and designed Home, Settings, Messages, Sign Up, AND Dark Mode. The entire app, without me asking. Built the whole thing on its own and burned through all my tokens. 😅
✅ Key lesson — When requesting designs from Claude, be explicit about "what" and "how far." Scope your prompt clearly.
Seeing that "come back in 24 hours" message was a little deflating... but honestly, I also felt the potential.
🔄 What I'm now rethinking
I'm planning to move my design system from Figma into Claude Design, and rebuild my workflow so that screen design, developer communication, and handoff all happen inside Claude.
Figma does support Claude integration — but when syncing with PMs and developers, showing them a working screen in real time is just faster.
It's still early and the limitations are real (tokens, for one), but this is the first time I've genuinely felt like my workflow could fundamentally shift.
To designers using AI in your actual day-to-day work — how are you making it work?
I write on LinkedIn partly to document what I'm testing, but mostly to ask questions and learn from others. I'd love to hear how you're working with AI on the ground. 🙌
