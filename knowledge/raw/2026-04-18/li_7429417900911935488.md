## 원문

Two of the fastest-growing forces shaping the global economy right now?
AI-powered content creation and AI-driven marketing. Every industry, from healthcare to entertainment and finance to tourism, is being reshaped by professionals who can think creatively and leverage AI as a co-creator, not just a tool.
The question is no longer whether students need these skills. It's how soon can we get them started.
That's exactly why we introduced two brand-new programs to our MIT International Science & Technology Initiatives (MISTI) Global Teaching Labs Bahrain 2026 lineup this year.
AI in Marketing & Content Creation taught students the full arc of modern marketing, from strategy and audience targeting to building live Instagram and TikTok campaigns, landing pages, promotional videos, and professional posters, all powered by AI tools like ChatGPT, Runway, CapCut, Canva AI, and Lovable.
AI Creators Lab took it further. Students with zero coding experience built AI-powered games, original soundtracks, web apps, chatbots, and automation workflows using tools like Rosebud AI, Suno, ElevenLabs, N8N, and Typebot. From idea to integrated product in just two weeks.
Nearly 60 students from both public and private schools across Bahrain, spanning different ages and backgrounds, proved that when you give young people the right tools and mentors, they don't just learn. They build.
These programs were led by Raj Mehta and Hannah Chung from Massachusetts Institute of Technology alongside Wadeea Rizwan, Sara Butt, and Qabas Al Hasni from the American University of Bahrain (AUBH). A powerful tri-institutional collaboration between Riffa Views International School, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, and American University of Bahrain (AUBH) that created something bigger than a program.
This is a startup concept that should be embedded in every school curriculum. The careers of tomorrow, AI strategists, prompt engineers, digital creators, automation designers, don't have traditional textbooks yet. But they do have programs like this.
We're not preparing students for the future. We're letting them build it.
