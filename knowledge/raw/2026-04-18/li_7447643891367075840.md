## 원문

Most companies don't have a spending problem.
They have a rules problem.
I've seen it at every stage from $5M to $50M ARR.
The team isn't overspending.
Nobody is being reckless.
But every month-end looks the same:
• Missing receipts
• Surprise invoices
• Slack threads that go nowhere
• Finance chasing approvals that should never have needed approval
And the fix everyone reaches for?
More approvals.
That's the wrong answer.
Here's what actually works:
Under $500 → auto-approved, no questions asked.
$500–$2K → manager signs off.
$2K+ → finance reviews.
That's it.
Three thresholds. Written down. Enforced the same way every time.
Add two more rules and you're done:
No receipt = no expense. No exceptions.
Exception? Predefined answer already exists. No Slack debate required.
The teams that move fastest aren't the ones with the loosest controls.
They're the ones whose rules are clear enough that nobody has to ask.
Most companies don't need more approvals.
They need better rules.
